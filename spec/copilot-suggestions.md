High Impact Fixes (Most Important)
1. Product pages are missing entirely
Leading footwear sites always include:
•	Multiple product photos
•	Price
•	Sizes
•	Colour options
•	Add to cart flow
•	Fit/comfort details
•	Materials
•	Reviews
Your “Shop” page only shows two images with buttons. This is the single biggest gap.
Fix: Create a proper product detail page template for Quickster and Quicktown.
2. Brand identity is unclear
The homepage says:
“Quicker Shoes is a UK footwear brand with over 30 years of manufacturing expertise…”
…but nothing else supports this claim.
Leading brands show:
•	Real photography
•	Factory or craftsmanship imagery
•	Brand story
•	Mission
•	Social proof
•	Lifestyle imagery
Fix: Add a strong brand story section, real imagery, and a value proposition.
3. Visual hierarchy is weak
The homepage has:
•	A large title
•	A “Shop now” button
•	A short paragraph
But the layout feels empty and lacks:
•	A hero image
•	A clear focal point
•	Supporting visuals
•	Contrast between sections
Fix: Add a hero image (shoe, lifestyle, or brand), and structure the page into clear sections.
4. Navigation labels are confusing
You have:
•	Shop
•	Quickster
•	Quicktown
But Quickster and Quicktown are product names, not categories. This breaks standard e commerce UX.
Leading sites use:
•	Men
•	Women
•	Kids
•	New
•	Sale
•	Collections
Fix: Move Quickster/Quicktown under “Shop” as product pages.
5. No trust or credibility elements
Missing:
•	Reviews
•	Testimonials
•	Shipping info
•	Returns policy
•	Secure checkout badges
•	Contact details beyond a form
Fix: Add a footer with:
•	Address
•	Email
•	Returns policy
•	Delivery info
•	Social links
This is essential for a business case study.
🥈 Medium Impact Fixes
6. Product naming inconsistency
On the Shop page:
•	Both products are labelled “Quicktown – Formal Shoes”
•	The sneaker is incorrectly labelled
Fix: Correct labels:
•	Quicktown — Formal Shoes
•	Quickster — Trainers
7. Design feels template based
Google Sites is fine for a prototype, but the design feels generic.
Leading brands use:
•	High quality photography
•	Strong typography
•	Custom colour palettes
•	Lifestyle imagery
Fix: Add:
•	A consistent colour palette
•	Better product photos
•	Section backgrounds
•	Icons for features (comfort, durability, waterproof, etc.)
8. Contact page is too minimal
It only says:
“If you have any questions…”
Leading brands include:
•	Customer service hours
•	Email
•	Phone
•	FAQ links
•	Returns info
Fix: Add more detail to make the brand feel real.
